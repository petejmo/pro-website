About me

I am a second-year linguistics PhD student at the University of Southern California.

Previously I studied linguistics and philosophy at the University of Minnesota Twin Cities.

My primary interest is in semantics, especially event semantics. I like formal theories of meaning that are rich enough to account for even subtle patterns of entailment. I'm also interested in how the nature of linguistic meaning relates to other systems of cognitive representation as well as reality. Right now I am working on a project about how the semantics of perception verbs and other depictive predicates condition their complement selection.
