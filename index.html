About me

I am a second-year linguistics graduate student at the University of Southern California.

Previously I studied linguistics and philosophy at the University of Minnesota.

I'm interested in semantics, especially event semantics. I like formal descriptions of meaning that are rich enough to fully realize the empirical facts about meaning relations in natural language. I'm interested in how the complex structure of linguistic meaning pertains to the other systems of cognitive representation and to reality. Right now I am working on a project about perception verbs and other depictive predicates and how their semantics relate to their complement selection patterns.

You can find out more about my research on my Output page.
