About me

I am a second-year linguistics PhD student at the University of Southern California.

Previously I studied linguistics and philosophy at the University of Minnesota.

My primary interest is in semantics, especially event semantics. I like formal descriptions of meaning that are rich enough to realize subtle empirical facts about natural language meaning relations. I'm curious about how the structure of linguistic meaning resembles or departs from other systems of cognitive representation as well as reality. Right now I am working on a project about how the semantics of perception verbs and other depictive predicates relate to their patterns of complement selection.
